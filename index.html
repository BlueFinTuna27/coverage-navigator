How to Navigate Florida's Disability Benefits System

Florida disability benefits run through DCF, APD, and Social Security — three agencies that rarely coordinate with each other. Most families find this out the hard way: after a letter arrives that doesn't explain what's actually happening.

1.4M+

people are denied Social Security disability benefits at initial application, every year — most for paperwork and evidence reasons that are fixable if you know what's being asked.

Source: SSA FY2025 disability workload data

This isn't a sign anything was done wrong. Initial decisions are often made from incomplete records, without anyone hearing directly from the applicant or their family. That's exactly where this guide is meant to help — before a letter turns into a missed deadline.

Inside: a plain-language explanation of whichever notice you've received, which of the three agencies actually sent it, and — for the hardest step, Social Security's review — exactly what the form is really asking and how to answer it.
